## IMMEDIATE ACTIONS

### 1. Supplier Contact (You need to do this)
**Contact these IndiaMART suppliers for quotes:**
- Search: https://dir.indiamart.com/impcat/kashmiri-saffron.html
- Ask for: Grade I Mongra saffron, GI-tagged, 500g batch quote
- Request: Certificate of Analysis (COA), GI certification
- Ask about: Export documentation, phytosanitary certificate

**Supplier red flags to watch:**
- Won't provide COA → walk away
- Price below ₹150/g → likely adulterated
- No GI tag → can't claim "Kashmir Saffron" in EU
- Won't send sample → no deal

### 2. Import Compliance Checklist
- [ ] **Customs:** Saffron HS code 0910.20 → 0% EU import duty ✓
- [ ] **Import VAT:** 19% Einfuhrumsatzsteuer (payable at customs)
- [ ] **Lab test:** Eurofins/Agrolab pesticide residue test (€80-150 per batch)
- [ ] **Food registration:** Register with local Lebensmittelüberwachungsamt
- [ ] **Traceability:** Batch number system (when/where harvested, who shipped)
- [ ] **Packaging:** Food-safe glass jars with tamper seal
- [ ] **Labeling:** Ingredients (100% Crocus sativus L.), origin (India/Kashmir), net weight, best-before date, batch number

## LEGAL REQUIREMENTS — DON'T SKIP
1. **Gewerbeanmeldung** — register your trade (online or at Bürgeramt)
2. **Impressum** — mandatory on any shop page (fines up to €50k)
3. **Widerrufsrecht** — 14-day return policy
4. **Food business registration** — notify your local health authority
5. **NO health claims** — sell as spice only, never "helps with X"
6. **Product liability insurance** — €200-400/year (strongly recommended)
